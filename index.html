1.2 Describing methods, skills and resources needed to complete digital projects successfully

Methods used to complete digital come in all shapes and sizes from waterfall which uses “eight Waterfall stages conception, initiation, analysis, design, construction, testing, implementation, and maintenance”. [2] To Agile which has itself had lots of variants such as SCRUM, Agile uses the principle of starting with a basic outline idea, then work on it in small teams or modules, teams then work in sprints and complete tasks to a time scale which can then be reviewed and shown to investors to highlight progress and development. Other methods include PRINCE2 and Extreme programming,these all go through a similar process, which according to Kasper Nymand a writer on forecast. it goes

  1. Scoping
  2. Planning
  3. Development
  4. Testing & Deployment
  5. Maintenance
Scoping is the brainstorming process, where ideas are evaluated and different options can be assessed for their viability. Planning is where more detail is added and ideas are fleshed out, requirements and user experience are brought into the picture. Development stage is where the rough elements are refined and development teams come in to test and expand key features. Testing and deployment involves doing a quality assessment and checking for final bugs and ironing out the last few problems before release for the user to use. Finally maintenance is used to keep the product supported and making sure it is stable.
Skills needed to create a digital project can vary, from knowing how to code using Javascript, ability to use HTML and CSS. Being able to understand how to use FTP (file transfer protocol) works and also social media to access millions for people to help grow the project. Other skills include being proficient in copywriting and editing. Skills can also be more general and can help teams organise and communicate, examples of this may be problem solving, negotiation and having an attention to detail.
Resources needed to create and run a digital project come in lots of shapes and foams, from software such as Trello which allows a team to plan and contribute to ideas in the planning stages. Other software such as Xcode help with design and coding software such as Visual Studio Code can create the back of house functions to bring your project to life.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) has been brought in to help protect Internet and software users data, it works on several areas to ensure customers have to give consent to how their data will be stored and processed. Sections where GDPR have been greatly increased to include, ‘Active opt-in’ where users will have to give consent, meaning they will have to tick a box to say whether accept how their data will be used. Next is ‘Granular’ where users will get all the information on how their data is processed. Then there is ‘`Unbundling’ where terms and conditions must be set apart from consent. This was followed by a ‘Not a precondition of service’ which means consent does not mean a precondition of signing up unless it is a newspaper subscription. Finally there is ‘Named’ which entails it should be clear you the user who is processing their data and this includes third party companies. All these aspects will impact on Digital projects, maybe causing users to avoid signing up, or the slow down of getting to the content causes users to try another platform however i believe having customers see this information helps them make more informed decisions about who and why their data is being used.
