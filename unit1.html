1.1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) has been brought in to help protect Internet and software users data, it works on several areas to ensure customers have to give consent to how their data will be stored and processed. Sections where GDPR have been greatly increased to include, ‘Active opt-in’ where users will have to give consent, meaning they will have to tick a box to say whether accept how their data will be used. Next is ‘Granular’ where users will get all the information on how their data is processed. Then there is ‘`Unbundling’ where terms and conditions must be set apart from consent. This was followed by a ‘Not a precondition of service’ which means consent does not mean a precondition of signing up unless it is a newspaper subscription. Finally there is ‘Named’ which entails it should be clear you the user who is processing their data and this includes third party companies. All these aspects will impact on Digital projects, maybe causing users to avoid signing up, or the slow down of getting to the content causes users to try another platform however i believe having customers see this information helps them make more informed decisions about who and why their data is being used.
